Craftsky moderation architecture

Ozone labeler lifecycle, from user report to enforced action

This diagram treats Ozone as Craftsky's moderation back office and labeler. Craftsky still owns product reads through the AppView, while reports and moderation decisions flow through atproto's labeler model.

02

Lifecycle Stages

1

User reports content

Flutter collects reason and optional details, then calls Craftsky AppView with only the Craftsky session token.

2

AppView validates

AppView confirms the subject exists, maps the reason, and keeps PDS OAuth tokens server-side.

3

PDS routes report

AppView calls com.atproto.moderation.createReport with proxy headers for the Craftsky labeler.

4

Ozone queues subject

Ozone stores the report as a private event and moves the subject into the unresolved review queue.

5

Moderator actions

A moderator acknowledges, comments, tags, escalates, labels, mutes, or marks the subject as taken down.

6

Craftsky enforces reach

AppView indexes labels/status and applies policy when serving timelines, profiles, threads, and notifications.

03

What Each System Owns

Craftsky AppView

Product boundary and enforcement

  • Authenticates the Craftsky session.
  • Validates report subjects against indexed records.
  • Uses stored PDS OAuth tokens for report forwarding.
  • Indexes labels and decides what Craftsky surfaces.
User PDS

User-owned speech layer

  • Stores user posts, profiles, likes, follows, and blobs.
  • Receives the report call from the server-held user session.
  • Routes the report to the configured labeler service.
  • Is not deleted from by Craftsky moderation actions.
Ozone Labeler

Moderation back office

  • Stores private report and action events.
  • Shows queues and subject history to moderators.
  • Publishes labels from the Craftsky labeler DID.
  • Provides audit trail and team workflow.
04

Action Outcomes

Ozone action What it means Craftsky enforcement
Acknowledge Report was reviewed and closed without a visible moderation label. No product change, but the report remains in Ozone history.
Escalate Subject needs a higher-confidence review or policy decision. No automatic user-visible change unless Craftsky adds an escalation hold policy.
Label Ozone publishes metadata such as spam, adult, harassment, or hide. AppView applies the configured policy: hide, blur, warn, or leave visible.
Mute in Ozone Temporarily keeps noisy repeated reports out of moderator queues. Moderator workflow only. It should not be confused with user-facing mutes.
Takedown Marks the subject as taken down by the Craftsky moderation service. Craftsky should stop surfacing the subject, while the PDS record still exists.
05

Implementation Cut Lines

First slice

Report post to Ozone MVP

  • Deploy dev or staging Ozone.
  • Configure Craftsky labeler DID.
  • Add Flutter report-post UX.
  • Add AppView report endpoint.
  • Forward reports with atproto-proxy to the labeler.
  • Verify reports appear in the Ozone unresolved queue.
Second slice

Label enforcement MVP

  • Subscribe to Ozone label stream.
  • Index active labels in AppView Postgres.
  • Define per-label visibility policy.
  • Filter or warn in timeline, profile posts, threads, and notifications.
  • Handle negated and expired labels.
  • Keep direct-post unavailable behavior consistent.
Important: Ozone does not automatically remove Craftsky content from the user's PDS. It creates moderation decisions and labels. Craftsky's AppView must consume those decisions and change reach in product APIs.
