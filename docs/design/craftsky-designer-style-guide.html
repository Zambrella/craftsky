CraftSky

Designer style guide

Paper cutout, warm craft, confident colour.

A practical handoff for the current CraftSky visual system: palette, type, components, motion, imagery, voice and asset references.

Built from docs/design/* and the Flutter theme in app/lib/theme/*.

Brand in one paragraph

  • Warm cream paper, never sterile grey SaaS.
  • Near-black ink rules and chunky outlines.
  • Cobalt is primary; electric red is used sparingly.
  • Photos sit on coloured paper rectangles.
  • Typography should feel editorial, tactile and maker-friendly.

Product principles: no ads, chronological feed, creator-owned data, transparent business accounts.

Colour Rules

Keep tight
  • No gradients.
  • No pure black. Use #161210.
  • No pure white backgrounds except cards, sheets and modals.
  • Cobalt is the only saturated blue.
  • Red is the only saturated red and should feel special.
  • Supporting swatches are backgrounds, never text colours.

Imagery

Photo direction
  • Warm natural light: morning or afternoon, not strip-lit studio.
  • Hands and material in-frame: yarn, fabric, paper patterns, cutting mats.
  • Real homes and real makers over slick studio setups.
  • Light grain and matte finish are welcome.
  • Use tight crops with craft detail. Avoid generic lifestyle stock.

Motion

Quiet pop
  • Fast UI feedback: 120ms.
  • Entrances: 220ms.
  • Modals: 320ms.
  • Default ease: cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1).
  • Pop ease: cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1).

Do and Do Not

Fast guardrails

Do

  • Use warm paper backgrounds and crisp ink borders.
  • Make display type large and editorial.
  • Use cobalt for primary action and selected state.
  • Put photos on coloured paper cutouts.
  • Use direct, plainspoken, slightly cheeky copy.

Do not

  • Do not add gradients, glassmorphism or blur-heavy chrome.
  • Do not use muted SaaS greys as core surfaces.
  • Do not use red everywhere. It should still punch.
  • Do not round everything softly; the system needs cut edges.
  • Do not use emoji in system chrome.
