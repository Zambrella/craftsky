A new home for makers.

A social platform for textile crafters on the open web. Post what you're making, find the patterns people are loving, follow your local yarn shop. No ads, no algorithm, no guessing.

    Posts, follows, everything — stored on the open AT Protocol. Not locked inside Craftsky, not ours to hold hostage.

Post yours. Find theirs.

When you post a project, you're not shouting into a feed — you're adding to a shared library of real makes. Your finished quilt helps someone else pick their next fabric. Your in-progress jumper shows another knitter how that pattern really fits.

And it works the other way. Need a new dress pattern? See every make crafters have posted. Curious how a fabric drapes? Find it on someone's actual project. Discoverability built for makers, by the makers using it.

Why we're building this.

The platforms crafters used to rely on aren't built for us anymore. They're built for engagement, for ads, for algorithms that decide who gets seen. Makers got squeezed out in the middle.

Craftsky is the alternative we wanted to exist: a chronological feed, search that actually finds things, no ads now or ever, and your data portable on the AT Protocol. A social platform where crafters are the point, not the audience being sold to.

The full vision doc is open for comments — take a look.

How it works.

  • Post once, own it forever.

    Every Craftsky post is stored on the open AT Protocol — a network of servers no single company controls. Your posts don't live in our database; we just read from the network.

  • We're the lens, not the landlord.

    Craftsky watches the network for craft projects, organises them, and shows them to you in chronological order. The data isn't ours — how it's presented is.

  • Move any time, take it all with you.

    If Craftsky isn't for you, point your account at another app on the network. Your followers come with you.

Who's behind it.

Craftsky is being built by Doug Todd — the developer behind Stash Hub — alongside anyone who wants to help shape it. The vision doc is open for comments and the code lives on GitHub, so nothing about how Craftsky gets built is a secret. We'd rather get it right than get it out fast.

Questions you might have.

When's it launching?

No dates yet. We'd rather get it right than get it out fast. Join the waiting list and we'll be in touch when there's something worth showing.

Will it be free?

Yes, and it stays free at the core. No ads, ever. Sustainability will probably come from optional premium features down the line. We'll be transparent about the plan when it's firm.

What's the AT Protocol?

It's the open network Bluesky is built on. Your posts live on a server you can move, your follower graph is portable, and no single company owns the data. If Craftsky disappears, your posts don't.

Can I use my Bluesky handle?

Yes. Sign in with your Bluesky handle and your follows come along. No second account to manage, no follower graph to rebuild.

Is this just for textile crafters?

To start, yes — sewing, knitting, crochet, quilting, embroidery. The posts are designed around those projects. Other crafts may follow, but we'd rather do one community well than spread thin.

How is this different from Instagram?

Instagram is built for engagement and advertisers. Craftsky is built for crafters: a chronological feed, no ads, real search, and the details you'd actually log about a project. Your data lives on an open network, so it's yours to keep or take.
