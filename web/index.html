The things we're building for.

A short list of what matters — and what we won't compromise on.

  • You're the user, not the product.

    No ads, ever. No data sold on the side. We'll figure out how to keep the lights on with you, in the open.

  • Your data is yours.

    Posts, follows, everything is stored on the open AT Protocol. Not locked inside Craftsky — yours to take with you.

  • Newest first. Always.

    No algorithm deciding what you see. You follow the people you want, and their posts show up in order.

  • Find the pattern, not the noise.

    Search by fabric, pattern, technique, or maker. The way you'd actually look for a make.

  • Open source. A platform you can help build.

    Craftsky is being built in the open, for the long haul. Feedback, ideas, and contributions from crafters are how it gets better.

Post yours. Find theirs.

When you post a project, you're adding to a shared library of real makes. Your finished quilt shows someone else how that fabric actually pieces together. Multiply that by every crafter posting, and you've got something no pattern photo can match.

And it works the other way. Need a new dress pattern? See every make crafters have posted. Curious how a fabric drapes? Find it on someone's actual project. Search built around how crafters look for things — not what an algorithm wants to show you.

Why we're building this.

The platforms crafters used to rely on aren't built for us anymore. They're built for engagement, for ads, for algorithms that decide who gets seen. Hashtags got killed off, search stopped working, and makers got squeezed out in the middle.

Craftsky is the alternative we wanted to exist: a chronological feed, search that actually finds things, no ads now or ever, and your data portable on the AT Protocol. A social platform where your creativity builds the community.

The full vision doc is open for comments — take a look.

How it works.

  • Post once, own it forever.

    Every Craftsky post is stored on the open AT Protocol — a network of servers no single company controls. Your posts don't live in our database; we just read from the network.

  • We're the lens, not the landlord.

    Craftsky tunes into the open network for craft posts and lays them out in order, newest first. The posts live on the open network. The way you see them is what we make.

  • Move any time, take it all with you.

    If Craftsky isn't for you, link your account to another app on the network. Your followers come with you.

Who's behind it.

Craftsky is being built by Doug Todd — the developer behind Stash Hub — alongside anyone who wants to help shape it. Feedback on the vision doc and pull requests on the code are both genuinely welcome — that's how Craftsky gets better.

Questions you might have.

When's it launching?

No dates yet. We'd rather get it right than get it out fast. Join the waiting list and we'll be in touch when there's something worth showing.

Will it be free?

Yes, and it stays free at the core. No ads, ever. Sustainability will probably come from optional premium features down the line. We'll be transparent about the plan when it's firm.

What's the AT Protocol?

It's the open network Bluesky is built on. Your posts live on a server you can move, your follower graph is portable, and no single company owns the data. If Craftsky disappears, your posts don't.

Can I use my Bluesky handle?

Yes. Sign in with your Bluesky handle and your follows come along. No second account to manage, no follower graph to rebuild.

Is this just for textile crafters?

To start, yes — sewing, knitting, crochet, quilting, embroidery. The posts are designed around those projects. Other crafts may follow, but we'd rather do one community well than spread thin.

How is this different from Instagram?

Instagram is built for engagement and advertisers. Craftsky is built for crafters: a chronological feed, no ads, real search, and the details you'd actually log about a project. Your data lives on an open network, so it's yours to keep or take.
